Mana symbols — *pips*, in designer slang — are the densest information channel on a Magic card. Five circles in the corner tell you a card's color, its cost, its deck-building constraints, and often its design intent before you read a word of rules text. If you're making custom cards, fluency in the full symbol vocabulary is the difference between a cost that looks printed and one that looks pasted on.

This is the complete vocabulary, symbol by symbol, with the rules meaning of each.

## Generic and variable costs

**`{0}` through `{20}`** — generic mana: payable with mana of any color (or colorless). A cost of `{3}{G}` means "any three, plus one green."

**`{X}`** (and the rare `{Y}` and `{Z}`) — variable costs chosen as the spell is cast. An `{X}{R}` Fireball-style spell can be cast for as much mana as you have. On a custom card, remember the rules convention: *"X is the amount of damage"* style clarifications go in the rules text only when the relationship isn't obvious.

## The five colors and colorless

**`{W}` `{U}` `{B}` `{R}` `{G}`** — white, blue (U, because B was taken), black, red, green. One pip = one mana of exactly that color.

**`{C}`** — explicitly *colorless* mana, introduced with the Eldrazi. `{C}` in a cost can only be paid with colorless mana — it is NOT generic. A custom card costed `{2}{C}` is meaningfully harder to cast than `{3}`.

## Hybrid: either/or pips

**The ten two-color hybrids — `{W/U}`, `{W/B}`, `{U/B}`, `{U/R}`, `{B/R}`, `{B/G}`, `{R/G}`, `{R/W}`, `{G/W}`, `{G/U}`** — are split discs payable with *either* color. A `{R/G}` pip is one mana that's red OR green, your choice per pip. Hybrid cards count as BOTH colors for color identity: a `{2}{W/U}` card is white-and-blue for deck-building purposes.

**Twobrids — `{2/W}`, `{2/U}`, `{2/B}`, `{2/R}`, `{2/G}`** — payable as two generic OR one of the color. A creature costing three twobrid white pips can be cast for `{6}`, for `{W}{W}{W}`, or anything between. Twobrid cards are the named color regardless of how you pay.

## Phyrexian: mana or life

**`{W/P}`, `{U/P}`, `{B/P}`, `{R/P}`, `{G/P}`** (and colorless `{C/P}`) — the phi symbol means the pip can be paid with the colored mana *or 2 life*. Phyrexian costs are infamous in competitive history precisely because "free" is a price players love; cost custom phyrexian designs conservatively. A phyrexian pip still makes the card that color.

## Utility symbols

These appear in rules text and activation costs rather than in the top-right cost line:

- **`{T}`** — the tap symbol: "tap this permanent" as a cost. *`{T}`: Add `{G}`.*
- **`{Q}`** — the untap symbol, `{T}`'s rare mirror: "untap this permanent" as a cost.
- **`{S}`** — snow mana: payable with any mana produced by a snow source. Deck-building texture, beloved of winter-themed custom sets.
- **`{E}`** — energy counters: not mana at all, but a resource symbol used in costs ("Pay `{E}{E}`") and payouts ("You get `{E}`").

## Color identity: what your pips imply

A card's **color identity** is every colored pip that appears anywhere on it — cost AND rules text. A creature with cost `{2}{U}` and an activated ability costing `{B}` has a blue-black identity (it can't go in a mono-blue Commander deck). When you design a custom card, audit the rules text for stray pips; an off-color activation is a real design choice with real deck-building consequences.

Two conventions worth following when you build:

1. **Symbol order**: generic first, then colors in WUBRG-wheel order starting from the card's "primary" color — `{2}{W}{U}`, not `{U}{W}{2}`.
2. **Curve honesty**: a double-pip cost like `{1}{R}{R}` is a real constraint — it asks the deck to be heavily red. Use doubled pips when you *want* the card to be hard to splash; use hybrid when you want it to travel between decks.

## FAQ

### What's the difference between colorless `{C}` and generic `{1}`?

Generic (`{1}`, `{2}`, …) accepts mana of any type. Colorless `{C}` demands specifically colorless mana — from a Wastes, an artifact source, an Eldrazi. Use `{C}` in a custom cost only when "must be colorless" is the point.

### Do hybrid pips make a card cheaper?

Effectively yes — hybrid is the *easier* cost shape because more decks can pay it. Designers usually compensate: hybrid cards are templated so they'd be fair as a mono-color card of either half.

### Can a custom card mix hybrid and phyrexian in one cost?

Mechanically yes (`{W/U}{B/P}` is parseable), and PipGlyph renders it — but real cards almost never do it, so expect it to read as deliberately exotic. Exotic is sometimes the brief.
